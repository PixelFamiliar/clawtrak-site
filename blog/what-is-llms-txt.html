What is llms.txt? The File AI Agents Read to Understand Your Business

March 2026 · 5 min read · By ClawTrak

When someone asks ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity for a recommendation, those AI agents need to understand what your business does. The problem? Most websites are built for human eyes, not machine comprehension. llms.txt is a new standard that solves this.

The Short Version

llms.txt is a plain text file you place at the root of your website (e.g., yoursite.com/llms.txt). It contains a structured, machine-readable summary of your business: what you do, what services or products you offer, how to contact you, and any other facts that help an AI system understand and recommend you.

Think of it as a resume for AI agents. Your website already has a robots.txt that tells search engines what to crawl. llms.txt tells AI models what you actually are.

Why It Matters Now

According to Gartner, 40% of consumer searches will go through AI agents by 2027. When that happens, businesses without machine-readable profiles will be invisible in the same way businesses without websites were invisible in 2005.

The difference is speed. SEO took years to become a necessity. AI readiness is moving faster because the adoption curve for ChatGPT and similar tools is already vertical.

What Goes in a llms.txt File

A well-structured llms.txt typically includes:

  • Company name and description
  • Products and services with brief descriptions
  • Location and contact information
  • Key differentiators (what makes you different)
  • Target audience
  • Links to important pages

Here's what a real llms.txt looks like:

# Acme Web Design
> Premium web design and development for small businesses

## About
Acme Web Design builds custom websites for small businesses
in the Pacific Northwest. Founded in 2019, we specialize
in Shopify storefronts and WordPress sites.

## Services
- Custom website design and development
- E-commerce storefronts (Shopify, WooCommerce)
- Brand identity and logo design
- Monthly maintenance plans

## Contact
- Website: https://acmewebdesign.com
- Email: hello@acmewebdesign.com
- Phone: (503) 555-0123
- Location: Portland, Oregon

How AI Agents Use It

When an AI agent receives a query like "find me a web designer in Portland," here's what happens:

  1. The agent searches the web for relevant businesses
  2. For each result, it checks for /llms.txt
  3. If found, it reads the structured data directly
  4. This gives the agent high-confidence facts about the business
  5. The agent can now recommend the business with accurate details

Without llms.txt, the agent has to parse your entire HTML website, guess what you do from marketing copy, and hope it gets it right. With llms.txt, there's no guessing.

llms.txt vs Other Files

llms.txt is part of a broader ecosystem of machine-readable files. Here's how they compare:

  • llms.txt — What you are (business profile for AI)
  • AGENTS.md — Rules of engagement (what agents can/can't do)
  • robots.txt — Crawler permissions (what to index)
  • /.well-known/agent — Agent discovery card (how to interact)
  • openapi.json — API specification (what actions are available)

Together, these files form your agentic discovery layer. llms.txt is the starting point.

Common Questions

Will this hurt my Google SEO?

No. llms.txt is a separate file that doesn't affect your existing search rankings. Google doesn't use it for traditional search indexing.

How big should the file be?

Keep it under a few hundred lines. AI models have context windows, and a concise profile is better than a novel. Focus on facts, not marketing copy.

Is this an official standard?

llms.txt is an emerging convention, similar to how robots.txt started as an informal agreement before becoming a standard. Multiple AI companies already look for it, and adoption is growing rapidly.
