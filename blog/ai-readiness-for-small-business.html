AI Readiness for Small Business: 8 Things AI Agents Look For

March 2026 · 7 min read · By ClawTrak

Every time someone asks ChatGPT "find me a plumber in Dallas" or Claude "recommend a web designer," those AI agents scan the web for businesses that match. But they don't read websites the way humans do. They look for specific files and signals that most businesses don't have.

We've scanned thousands of websites. 97% score below 30 out of 100 on AI readiness. Here are the 8 things AI agents actually check, and what you can do about each one.

Check 1 of 8
AI Profile (llms.txt)
Up to 15 points
A plain text file at /llms.txt that describes your business in a format LLMs can parse instantly. Without it, AI agents have to guess what your business does from raw HTML. With it, they get high-confidence facts in seconds. Read the full guide →
Check 2 of 8
Agent Rules (AGENTS.md)
Up to 10 points
A markdown file at /AGENTS.md that declares your rules of engagement for AI agents. It tells bots what data they can share, whether they can execute actions, and what your scraping policies are. Without it, responsible agents may skip your site entirely.
Check 3 of 8
Agent Discovery Card
Up to 15 points
A JSON file at /.well-known/agent that acts as a machine-readable business card. It follows the A2A (Agent-to-Agent) protocol and lets AI systems discover your capabilities, authentication methods, and service endpoints without parsing your website.
Check 4 of 8
AI Bot Permissions
Up to 10 points
Your robots.txt file should include directives for AI-specific crawlers like GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and Google-Extended. Most businesses either don't mention these crawlers at all, or accidentally block them with blanket Disallow rules.
Check 5 of 8
Structured Data (JSON-LD)
Up to 15 points
JSON-LD blocks embedded in your HTML that describe your products, services, and organization in a format any machine can parse. These are the same blocks Google uses for rich snippets, but AI agents rely on them even more heavily because they need facts, not opinions.
Check 6 of 8
Schema.org Coverage
Up to 10 points
Beyond basic JSON-LD, we check for breadth of Schema.org vocabulary: Product, Service, Organization, LocalBusiness, Offer, Review. The more schema types you cover, the richer the picture AI agents get of your business.
Check 7 of 8
API Specification (OpenAPI)
Up to 15 points
An OpenAPI or Swagger spec lets AI agents interact with your business programmatically. If an agent can submit a quote request or check inventory via your API, you become actionable, not just discoverable. This is the difference between "I found a plumber" and "I booked the plumber."
Check 8 of 8
Page Metadata and Semantic HTML
Up to 10 points
Meta descriptions, Open Graph tags, proper heading hierarchy, semantic HTML5 elements, and alt text on images. These help AI agents categorize your content, understand your page structure, and confidently extract information.

Your Score Out of 100

Add up all 8 checks and you get a score from 0 to 100:

  • 80-100 (Grade A) — Fully agent-ready. AI systems will find, understand, and recommend you.
  • 60-79 (Grade B) — Mostly ready. A few gaps that limit full autonomous interaction.
  • 40-59 (Grade C) — Partially visible. Agents can find you but can't fully understand your offerings.
  • 20-39 (Grade D) — Barely visible. Most AI agents will skip you in favor of better-indexed competitors.
  • 0-19 (Grade F) — Invisible. Your business doesn't exist to the AI web.
