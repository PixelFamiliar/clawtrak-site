Born from OpenClaw. Built because we saw what was missing.

While building Pixel Familiar, our autonomous AI agent, we gave it a simple job: go find services, evaluate them, and transact on our behalf. It couldn't. Almost every business it encountered was invisible. Websites for human eyes. Checkout flows requiring mouse clicks. No metadata, no API surface, no way for a machine to understand the offering.

Web 1.0 was read. Web 2.0 was write. Web 3.0 was own. Web 4.0 is delegate. AI agents are becoming autonomous participants in commerce. The overwhelming majority of businesses aren't prepared.

We don't write production backend code. We don't need access to your databases. We engineer Agentic Discovery and Scaffolding Layers: the structured data files that make your existing infrastructure readable, citable, and trustworthy to machines. Your engineers keep full control of deployment.

Operating safely in the age of AI scraping.

Canada's federal AI act (AIDA) died on the Order Paper in January 2025. But provincial and international enforcement didn't stop. Quebec's Law 25 carries massive fines for undisclosed automated profiling. The EU AI Act requires AI system transparency for any business serving European clients.

ClawTrak generates the structured files that help enterprises explicitly declare their data provenance, AI scraping consent, and privacy guardrails to visiting agents. Strategic robots.txt policies, AGENTS.md rules, and machine-readable compliance disclosures that keep you defensible under PIPEDA, Law 25, and EU regulations.

Not legal advice. Architectural governance. The difference between hoping AI respects your terms of service and engineering deterministic rules that enforce them.
